## The Problem With the Current Version

1. **Breathing-heavy**: Sections 1, 3 are almost entirely about breath/HRV. The equation applies to neural oscillations, quantum measurement, memory reconsolidation, death, ancient architecture, cross-cultural traditions, psychedelic states — but the paper reads like a breathwork manifesto.

2. **Story buried**: Section 8 is after all the evidence. By then, a reader has already decided whether they care. The story IS the hook — not as ego, but as methodology. How does a CS coach independently map the same territory that 190 papers describe? That question makes someone keep reading.

3. **Death is one sentence**: "Brain death isn't the end of consciousness" in Section 9. But NDE research is peer-reviewed (van Lommel in Lancet, Parnia's AWARE study). This belongs in the evidence, not as a throwaway implication.

4. **Fence-sitting tone**: "Challenge us. Tell us what we're missing." reads as asking permission. If the finding stands, state the finding.

5. **No cross-cultural contemplative evidence**: The paper mentions "every culture" but doesn't cite the evidence (Lutz et al. gamma synchrony, Hood's M-Scale, Griffiths psilocybin mystical experience).

6. **Publishing plan in the paper**: This is internal planning, not for readers.
