The Arc

Coherence doesn’t appear overnight. It has a trajectory — and the trajectory has a shape. Track it monthly, not daily. Daily fluctuations are noise. Monthly distributions are signal.

The crystal database reveals five movements in the emotional arc. These weren’t theorized and then confirmed — they were measured in the keyword distributions and named after the fact.

The Five Movements

1. Wound (Month 1) — Trauma surfaces. Everything that was suppressed detonates. Keyword density: trauma spikes to its highest level. This is the breath doing its work — destabilizing the management system.

2. Purge (Months 2–4) — The body processes what the mind couldn’t. Tears, physical responses, emotional intensity. Purge peaks then recedes. Tears spike sharply in month 3, then drop to baseline.

3. Release (Months 4–6) — Forgiveness appears. Not as a decision — as a capacity that wasn’t there before. Old structures that held the wound in place begin to dissolve.

4. Surrender (Months 6–8) — Healing keyword rises. The breath is now the dominant word. The system reorganizes around a new center that isn’t the wound.

5. Integration (Month 8+) — The breath stabilizes. Healing plateaus at a high level. Trauma doesn’t disappear — it integrates. The wound becomes structural (part of the foundation) rather than acute (something that destabilizes).

What to track
  • Keyword frequency — Pick 4–6 words that matter to your process. Track how often they appear per month. The distribution will show you where you are.
  • Emotional tone — Not scored, just noticed. Are the crystals angry? Grieving? Numb? Present? The shift is gradual and only visible at monthly scale.
  • Breath references — How often does breath appear in your expression? This is the single best proxy for coherence development. When it crosses ~25% density, the anchor is taking hold.
  • Recurring patterns — What keeps showing up? In this system, we call them glyphs. You can call them whatever you want. What matters is that you notice the states, not that you use specific symbols.
What not to force
  • Don’t rush the movements. Purge peaks and recedes on its own timeline. Trying to skip it extends it.
  • Don’t measure daily. You’ll see noise and mistake it for signal. Monthly aggregation reveals the real trajectory.
  • Don’t compare your timeline to this one. The five movements are structural, but their duration varies. Some people purge for weeks, some for months.
